Architectural drawings in Fulham — what you need to know

Fulham occupies some of the most sought-after residential streets in south-west London. The SW6 postcode, administered by the London Borough of Hammersmith and Fulham, is characterised by long runs of Victorian terraced housing interspersed with larger villa properties around Parsons Green and Hurlingham. Growing families and rising property values drive relentless demand for extensions, basement conversions and loft additions — making Fulham one of our busiest neighbourhoods for architectural drawing commissions.

Victorian terraces in Parsons Green and Munster Village: The streets radiating from Parsons Green and through Munster Village contain some of London's finest mid-Victorian terraced houses. These properties typically feature three storeys plus a semi-basement, bay windows to the front, and a rear addition with a side return. They share consistent structural characteristics that we know intimately — masonry party walls, timber floors, and pitched slate roofs with adequate ridge height for loft conversion. The side return presents the classic opportunity for a ground-floor kitchen extension, while the existing semi-basement often has headroom suitable for formal habitable use with appropriate tanking and light-well improvements.

Basement conversions: Fulham has become one of London's basement conversion hotspots. The combination of high property values, limited above-ground extension potential on mid-terrace sites, and generally favourable ground conditions makes sub-surface extension economically rational. Hammersmith and Fulham Council requires a detailed structural method statement, a construction management plan, a drainage impact assessment and often a party wall schedule. We produce the complete architectural documentation package and coordinate with structural and drainage engineers to ensure a smooth application process.

Conservation areas: Fulham has several designated conservation areas including Parsons Green, Munster Village, Walham Grove, Moore Park and the Hurlingham area. These areas restrict permitted development rights, meaning most external alterations — including many rear extensions, loft dormers and changes to fenestration — require full planning permission. Our team regularly prepares heritage-sensitive designs and supporting Heritage Statements that satisfy the council's conservation officers. We understand the design parameters the council expects: matching materials, sympathetic proportions, and minimal impact on the established streetscape character.

Family homes driving extension demand: Fulham's popularity with families means constant demand for additional living space. The most common commissions we handle are rear extensions creating open-plan kitchen-diners, loft conversions adding a master bedroom suite, and basement digs providing a media room or additional bedroom. Many homeowners combine two or three of these projects into a single comprehensive refurbishment — our Complete package covers the full scope from planning through building regulations in one fixed-fee engagement.

Loft conversions: Fulham's Victorian roofs generally offer good ridge height for conversion. The most common approach on terraced streets is a rear dormer, often extending across the full width of the main roof. L-shaped dormers — wrapping around the outrigger — are popular where maximum floor area is the priority. Within conservation areas, Hammersmith and Fulham Council typically requires set-back dormers clad in materials that match the existing roof, with a preference for lead or zinc over fibre-cement tiles.

How much do architectural drawings cost in Fulham?

Our fixed-fee planning drawing package for Fulham starts at £840. Extensions and loft conversions from £1,225. Full building regulations packages from £1,750. Basement conversions are quoted bespoke due to the additional structural and drainage documentation required.

Can I do a basement conversion in Fulham?

Yes — basement conversions are popular in Fulham due to high property values and limited above-ground extension potential on some sites. Hammersmith and Fulham Council permits single-storey basements beneath the main footprint subject to a structural method statement, drainage impact assessment and construction management plan. We produce the complete architectural and supporting documentation package.

Are there conservation areas in Fulham?

Yes — Fulham has several conservation areas including Parsons Green, Munster Village, Walham Grove, Moore Park and Hurlingham. Properties within these areas face additional design scrutiny and restricted permitted development rights. Our team regularly prepares heritage-sensitive applications with supporting Heritage Statements.

Do I need planning permission for an extension in Fulham?

It depends on the size and location. Single-storey rear extensions up to 3 metres (terraced) may qualify as permitted development outside conservation areas. Within conservation areas, or for larger extensions, full planning permission is required. We check your specific property's constraints before recommending the best route.
